Policies overview or signature issue for each candidate is from their campaign website or from The Scope's interviews with the candidates.

Running for mayor

Andrea Campbell

Currently Boston city councilor representing District 4, previously city council president

  • Has served on the city council for five years.
  • Has focused on issues surrounding criminal justice, policing reform and racial justice while in office.
  • Filed an ordinance in July, 2020 with two other councilors to create a civilian review board with authority to review, investigate and resolve complaints of police misconduct submitted by the public or other officers and personnel.
  • Was the first Black woman to serve as the City Council president and if elected would be first woman and person of color to be Boston Mayor; challenged Marty Walsh before he was nominated as Labor Secretary
  • Read Campbell's interview with the Scope

  • See Campbell's campaign platform

John Barros

Former Chief of Economic Development under Marty Walsh’s administration

  • Former executive director of the Dudley Street Neighborhood Initiative, member of the Boston School Committee under Mayor Menino’s administration and co-owner of Restaurante Cesaria in Dorchester.
  • Priorities include reopening the economy in a safe and equitable way, creating more affordable housing, and making sure public schools work for Boston students.
  • Ran for mayor in 2013, a race in which he placed sixth out of twelve candidates.
  • Read Barros's interview with the Scope
    •
  • See Janey's campaign platform
    •

Kim Janey

Incumbent acting mayor of Boston, previously city council president

  • First woman and the first Black mayor in the city’s history and first woman to represent District 7 on the council.
  • Has served in the council since 2017.
  • As a councilor, she has focused on education and social justice and said in her campaign announcement video that her core issues would include tackling the housing and racial wealth gap, education and transit inequality and disparities worsened by COVID-19.
  • Named one of Boston’s Most Impactful Black Women in 2021.
  • Read Janey's mayoral bid announcement on the Scope
    •
  • See Janey's campaign platform
    •

Annissa Essaibi George

Currently Boston city councilor at-large

  • Has served on the city council since Nov. 2015.
  • Has worked as a teacher for 13 years and is also the owner of Stitch House in Dorchester, a store that sells yarn and fabrics.
  • Priorities in city council have revolved around education, homelessness, and mental health
  • If elected would be the first woman and person of color to be Boston Mayor.
  • Read Essaibi George's interview with the Scope
    •

Jon Santiago

Democratic Massachusetts state representative for the 9th Suffolk district since 2019. ER Doctor at Boston Medical Center.

  • Has been serving as the Massachusetts state representative for the 9th Suffolk district since 2019.
  • Formerly a volunteer in the Peace Corps and captain in the U.S. Army Reserve.
  • Priorities include tackling the disparity across health, education, people’s livelihoods and COVID-19 related issues, including equitable vaccine distribution, school reopening and support for small businesses.
  • Read Santiago's interview with the Scope
    •
  • See Santiago's campaign platform
    •

Michelle Wu

Currently Boston city councilor at-large, previously city council president

  • Has served on the city council for seven years and was the first Asian-American woman to serve on the Boston City Council. As a city councilor, Wu has championed issues surrounding housing inequality, transportation and climate change.
  • Has championed issues surrounding housing inequality, transportation and climate change.
  • Has advocated for free public transportation in Boston, proposed a Green New Deal and Just Recovery plan for Boston and has pushed to abolish the Boston Planning and Development Agency to give residents more power over developments.
  • If elected would be first woman and person of color to be Boston Mayor and the first Mayor born outside of Boston; challenged Marty Walsh before he was nominated as Labor Secretary

Kelly Bates

Founding executive director of the Elma Lewis Center for Civic Engagement, Learning, and Research. Founding board member of Emerge Massachusetts, an organization that trains and supports women running for political office.

  • Served on Mayor Menino’s School Choice Committee to work toward quality education for children in Boston.
  • Is a member of the Ward 18 Democratic Committee who organizes communities in Hyde Park, Mattapan, and Roslindale and promotes civic engagement

Lydia Edwards

Incumbent city councilor, District 1

  • As councilor, Edwards has pushed for oversight of the council budget through city charter amendment, advocated for public transit expansion to protect the environment and prevent traffic congestion, and implemented a fee on houses sold for over $2 million, contributing to affordable housing.
  • Priorities include supporting affordable housing, climate justice, and public education for communities of color.
  • Currently working against an electric substation that would damage the climate resiliency measures of East Boston
  • Was a public interest labor attorney and campaign coordinator for the Massachusetts Coalition for Domestic Workers prior to her council tenure
